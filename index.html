About the Event

Where Engineers Build the Future

Prototype Challenge is the flagship technical event of Advyka 2026, the annual techfest of College of Engineering Perumon, organized by IEDC CEP — the Innovation & Entrepreneurship Development Cell supported by Kerala Startup Mission.

Participants bring working prototypes — hardware, software, or hybrid — and present them to industry professionals in a single-day intensive showcase. Projects are evaluated on innovation, real-world impact, technical depth, and presentation.

  • 🚀
    Open to all disciplines — EEE, CSE, ME, Civil, Biotech & more
  • 🤝
    Teams of 2–4 — Collaborate and compete together
  • 🏛️
    Inter-college — Open to students across Kerala
  • 💡
    KSUM Mentorship — Get feedback from startup mentors

Guidelines

Rules & Eligibility

Read carefully before registering. All participants must follow these guidelines.

01

Team Size

Teams must have 2–4 members. Solo entries are not accepted for this event.

02

Eligibility

Open to all undergraduate & postgraduate students from recognized colleges in Kerala.

03

Working Prototype Required

A functional prototype must be physically present. PPT-only or simulation-only entries will not be evaluated.

04

Originality

Projects must be original work. Plagiarism or purchased kits presented as original will result in disqualification.

05

Registration Fee

₹300 per team (non-refundable). Payment via Razorpay during registration. Confirmation email sent instantly.

06

ID Proof Required

All team members must carry valid college ID cards on the day of the event for entry verification.

07

Power & Space

Each team gets a 4×4 ft table + one 5A socket. Additional power requirements must be declared at registration.

08

Judges' Decision

The judging panel's decision is final and binding. No appeals after results are announced.

Evaluation

Judging Criteria

Projects evaluated across six dimensions by industry professionals and KSUM mentors.

💡
25%
Innovation
How novel is the idea? Does it solve a problem in an unconventional way?
⚙️
20%
Technical Depth
Complexity, implementation quality, and understanding of the technology used.
🌍
20%
Real-World Impact
Does the prototype address a genuine problem with tangible potential impact?
🎯
15%
Functionality
Does the prototype actually work as demonstrated? Reliability matters.
🎤
12%
Presentation
Clarity, confidence, and ability to explain and sell the idea to judges.
📈
8%
Scalability
Can this prototype realistically be taken to a product or startup?
